## Scope
Stroke publishes original research, reviews, and clinical studies on cerebral circulation and cerebrovascular disease, including risk assessment, diagnosis, prevention, acute treatment, rehabilitation, and basic vascular biology. Coverage spans ischemic and hemorrhagic stroke, intracerebral and subarachnoid hemorrhage, small vessel disease, vascular cognitive impairment, cerebrovascular imaging, large randomized trials, epidemiology, and translational neurovascular science. It is the AHA/ASA flagship journal for stroke medicine.

## Special Notes
Stroke is the highest-impact journal dedicated to cerebrovascular disease and is the primary publication venue for AHA/ASA stroke guidelines, major stroke trials (thrombolysis, thrombectomy, secondary prevention), and large cohort or registry analyses. It emphasizes rigorous methodology and studies likely to inform practice at the guideline level, and accepts both clinical and basic neurovascular science. Choose Stroke over JNIS when the study addresses broader stroke pathophysiology, epidemiology, pharmacological treatment, rehabilitation, or trial-level clinical outcomes rather than neurointerventional procedures or device performance. AI policy: follows AHA editorial policy aligned with ICMJE — authors must disclose use of AI or AI-assisted technologies (including large language models) in the Methods section, and AI tools cannot be listed as authors.
