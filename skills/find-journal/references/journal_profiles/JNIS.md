## Scope
JNIS publishes original research, reviews, case series, technical notes, and case reports on endovascular and minimally invasive neurointerventional procedures. Coverage includes acute ischemic stroke intervention, cerebral aneurysm treatment (coiling, flow diversion, WEB, intrasaccular devices), AVM and dural AVF embolization, carotid and intracranial stenting, venous sinus stenting, spinal vascular intervention, tumor embolization, emerging neurovascular devices, and AI/ML applications in neurointerventional workflow.

## Special Notes
JNIS is the official journal of the Society of NeuroInterventional Surgery (SNIS) and a partner journal of multiple international neurointerventional societies. It is the highest-impact journal dedicated specifically to neurointerventional surgery and the primary venue for stroke thrombectomy trials, flow-diverter outcome studies, and major neurovascular device evaluations. Strong preference for clinically validated outcome data and multi-center registry studies for major device or technique claims. Technical videos are a distinctive accepted format. Choose JNIS over INSI when the study carries high-impact clinical outcome data (stroke outcomes, aneurysm occlusion, device pivotal trials) rather than technique-only or single-institution case reports. AI policy: follows BMJ policy — disclosure required in Methods and Acknowledgments; all tasks permitted if disclosed; applies to text, images, data, and diagrams.
