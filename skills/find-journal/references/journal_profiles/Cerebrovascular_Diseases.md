## Scope
Cerebrovascular Diseases publishes original research, reviews, systematic reviews, brief reports, and study protocols on stroke and cerebrovascular disease, covering prevention, diagnosis, management, rehabilitation, and related fields of molecular genetics, vascular biology, pathophysiology, epidemiology, and health systems science. It welcomes clinically oriented smaller studies, imaging-based investigations, and mechanistic vascular biology work alongside larger cohort analyses.

## Special Notes
Cerebrovascular Diseases is a long-established Karger journal with broad international readership in stroke medicine and cerebrovascular science. It is receptive to mid-sized clinical studies, imaging-based work, vascular biology, and systematic reviews that may not compete at Stroke's trial-heavy bar, and a Statements section (Ethics, Conflict of Interest, Funding, Author Contributions, Data Availability) is mandatory; a graphical abstract is recommended and a plain language summary (≤250 words) is optional. Choose Cerebrovascular Diseases over Stroke when the study is a mid-sized imaging, observational, or mechanistic cerebrovascular investigation that values a receptive international venue over the trial-dominated profile of Stroke; the companion journal Cerebrovascular Diseases Extra absorbs fully open-access submissions. AI policy: follows Karger editorial policy — mandatory disclosure of any use of large language models or other generative AI tools in study conduct or manuscript preparation, declared in the Methods section (or Acknowledgements if no Methods section is present); AI cannot be listed as an author.
