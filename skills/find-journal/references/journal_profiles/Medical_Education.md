## Scope
Premier journal for scholarship in health professions education, publishing conceptually grounded research across undergraduate, postgraduate, and continuing education. Equally welcomes quantitative and qualitative studies but demands explicit conceptual frameworks in research introductions.

## Scope Keywords
medical education research, conceptual framework, health professions education, clinical reasoning, assessment, curriculum, qualitative research, workplace-based learning, professional identity, competency-based medical education, faculty development, learner well-being, realist evaluation, programmatic assessment, educational theory

## Special Notes
Top-tier medical education journal with <10% acceptance rate. Titles must be ≤15 words; manuscripts require both full and anonymized versions (double-blind review). Conceptual/theoretical framework mandatory in Research introductions. AI policy: AI tools cannot be listed as author; disclosure encouraged when AI use is fundamental to research.
