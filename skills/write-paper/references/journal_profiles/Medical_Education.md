## Abstract Format

Structured under subheadings, maximum 300 words.
Not required for: Really Good Stuff, When I Say..., Correspondence.

## Keywords

Not explicitly specified; optimize for search engine discoverability.

## Required Sections

For Research articles (IMRAD):
1. **Introduction** (must include strong conceptual framework and gap in knowledge)
2. **Methods**
3. **Results**
4. **Discussion** (including strengths and weaknesses)

For Really Good Stuff (3 sections):
1. What problem was addressed?
2. What was tried?
3. What lessons were learned?

For Focus on Research Methods:
1. Background
2. Process
3. Pearls

## Citation Style

- Vancouver (numbered) style
- Double-spaced references
- Preprints: include "[preprint]" designation
- Data citations follow Joint Declaration of Data Citation Principles

## Reporting Guidelines

- Follow ICMJE Recommendations
- Ethics approval required (or explicit statement that none was needed)
- Declaration of Helsinki compliance
- COPE Code of Conduct

## Special Notes

- **Title**: 15 words or fewer (90 characters including spaces); editor may edit.
- **ORCID required** for all authors.
- **Anonymous manuscript required**: submit both full and fully anonymized versions.
- **Active voice** preferred; short sentences and clear subheadings.
- **Qualitative and quantitative equally welcome**.
- **Conceptual framework mandatory** in Research introductions.
- **AI policy**: AI tools cannot be listed as author; authors responsible for AI-generated content accuracy; disclosure encouraged when AI use is fundamental to research.
- **Really Good Stuff**: max 4 authors, 1 reference, no figures/tables.
- **Decision timeline**: initial decision < 1 month; nearly all within 12 weeks.
- Report both numbers and percentages (not percentages alone).
- Figures embedded at end of manuscript in single document preferred.
