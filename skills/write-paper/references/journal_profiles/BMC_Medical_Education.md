# BMC Medical Education

## Journal Identity

- **Full name**: BMC Medical Education
- **Abbreviation**: BMC Med Educ
- **Publisher**: BioMed Central (Springer Nature)
- **ISSN**: 1472-6920
- **Frequency**: Continuous (online only)
- **Impact Factor**: ~3.6 (2024)
- **Open Access**: Yes (CC BY 4.0)
- **APC**: ~$2,490

## Manuscript Types and Word Limits

| Type | Abstract | Manuscript Body | Figures | Tables |
|------|----------|----------------|---------|--------|
| Research Article | 350 | No strict limit (typically 3000-5000) | No limit | No limit |
| Study Protocol | 350 | No strict limit | No limit | No limit |
| Systematic Review | 350 | No strict limit | No limit | No limit |

Word counts exclude abstract, references, figure legends, and tables.
Pilot/feasibility studies should be submitted as Research Articles (not Study Protocols).

## Abstract Format

Structured with four headings:
1. **Background**
2. **Methods**
3. **Results**
4. **Conclusions**

Maximum 350 words. No abbreviations if possible. No references in abstract.
Include trial registration info if applicable.

## Keywords

3-10 keywords after abstract.

## Required Sections

1. **Background** (replaces Introduction)
2. **Methods**
3. **Results**
4. **Discussion**
5. **Conclusions** (can be subsection of Discussion or separate)

Additional required:
- **Declarations**: Ethics, Consent, Data availability, Competing interests, Funding, Authors' contributions
- **Abbreviations** list
- **Supplementary information** (if any)

## Citation Style

- Vancouver (numbered) style
- Numbered sequentially in order of first appearance
- Use square brackets [1] in text (not superscript)
- List all authors (no "et al." cutoff for BMC journals)
- Format: Author(s). Title. Journal Abbreviation. Year;Vol(Issue):Pages. doi:

## Reporting Guidelines

- Observational studies: STROBE
- Educational quality improvement: SQUIRE 2.0
- Pilot/feasibility: follow CONSORT extension for pilot trials if applicable
- Completed checklist must be uploaded at submission

## Special Notes for Pilot Studies

- Frame as feasibility/acceptability study, not efficacy
- Emphasize: recruitment, retention, instrument reliability, effect size estimation
- Sample size calculation for future full-scale study is expected
- Do not overclaim based on underpowered p-values
